### Interpretation

Without any January data (jan_0), the model falls back on the AR(1) prior and estimates roughly zero job growth with enormous uncertainty (\~3,800K wide 80% HDI).

Adding G's January signal (jan_1) shifts the median **negative** to −131K. G's actual January observation was **−3.7% growth** — a sharp contraction signal that pulls the estimate below the prior-based backcast. The 80% HDI width barely changes (3,819K → 3,837K); G shifts the point estimate but doesn't reduce uncertainty.

The official CES 1st print (+130K) was far from G's signal. G disagreed with CES on this month's direction. Without CES, the model has \~3,800K of uncertainty about a single month — CES is the primary source of identification for monthly employment.

All structural parameters stable across runs (phi ≈ 0.90, tau ≈ 0.011, lambda_CES ≈ 0.97). Convergence clean (0–2 divergences).
